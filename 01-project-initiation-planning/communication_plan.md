# Communication Plan
## Laser Print Template Layout Management System

**Project Name:** Laser Print Template Layout Management System
**Date:** 2025-12-18
**Version:** 1.0

---

## 1. Communication Objectives

The communication plan ensures:
- All stakeholders receive timely, relevant project information
- Clear escalation paths for issues and decisions
- Transparent progress tracking and status reporting
- Effective collaboration across teams
- Stakeholder expectations are managed appropriately

---

## 2. Communication Principles

1. **Transparency:** Share information openly with appropriate stakeholders
2. **Timeliness:** Communicate updates promptly, especially for critical issues
3. **Relevance:** Tailor messages to audience needs and interests
4. **Two-way:** Encourage feedback and questions
5. **Consistency:** Use standard formats and channels
6. **Accessibility:** Ensure information is easy to find and understand

---

## 4. Communication Types and Templates

### 4.1 Project Status Report

**Audience:** Steering Committee, Project Sponsor
**Frequency:** Monthly
**Owner:** Project Manager
**Distribution Method:** Email + Project Repository

**Content:**
- Executive Summary
- Overall Project Status (RAG: Red/Amber/Green)
- Accomplishments This Period
- Planned Activities Next Period
- Key Milestones Status
- Budget Status
- Top 3 Risks
- Top 3 Issues
- Decisions Required
- Change Requests

**Format:** 2-page written report + 10-slide presentation (if meeting)

---

### 4.2 Weekly Team Status Update

**Audience:** Project Team
**Frequency:** Weekly
**Owner:** Project Manager
**Distribution Method:** Email

**Content:**
- This Week's Accomplishments
- Next Week's Priorities
- Blockers/Issues
- Team Member Updates
- Upcoming Meetings/Deadlines
- Action Items

**Format:** Email (1 page)

---

### 4.3 Daily Standup (Development Phase)

**Audience:** Development Team, BA, QA
**Frequency:** Daily during development
**Owner:** Project Manager / Scrum Master
**Distribution Method:** In-person or video call (15 min)

**Content (per person):**

- What I'm working on today

---

### 4.4 Sprint Review/Demo

### 4.6 Technical Review Meeting

## 7. Escalation Path and Response Times

| Severity | Description | Escalation Level | Response Time | Notification Method |
|----------|-------------|------------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Critical** | Project blocker, production down | Level 3: Sponsor | 1 hour | Phone + Email |
| **High** | Major risk, timeline impact | Level 2: Steering Committee | 4 hours | Email + Meeting |
| **Medium** | Issue needs attention | Level 1: Project Manager | 1 business day | Email |
| **Low** | Minor issue, FYI | Team Level | 3 business days | Email or team meeting |

**Escalation Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Project Manager
- **Level 2:** Steering Committee
- **Level 3:** Project Sponsor / Executive Management

## 10. Communication Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| Information overload | Stakeholders tune out | Tailor communication to audience; use summaries |
| Communication gaps | Misalignment, surprises | Use multiple channels; confirm receipt |
| Language/jargon barriers | Misunderstanding | Use plain language; define technical terms |
| Time zone differences (Hertwich) | Delayed responses | Schedule meetings considering time zones; use async communication |
| Lack of engagement | Missing requirements | Make communication interactive; solicit feedback |
| Over-communication | Meeting fatigue | Be concise; combine meetings where possible |

---

## 11. Feedback Mechanisms

1. **Open door policy:** Team members can raise concerns anytime with PM or BA
2. **Anonymous feedback:** Suggestion box or online form
3. **Retrospectives:** Continuous improvement of communication process
4. **Surveys:** Periodic stakeholder satisfaction surveys
5. **Q&A sessions:** Dedicated time in meetings for questions

## 13. Success Metrics

Communication plan effectiveness will be measured by:
- Stakeholder satisfaction survey results (target: >4/5)
- Number of surprises/miscommunications (target: <2 per phase)
- Meeting attendance rates (target: >90% for required attendees)
- Response time to communication (target: within defined SLAs)
- Clarity of information (measured in feedback surveys)
